What is an SWP Calculator?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) calculator is a simple online tool that allows you to estimate the prospective withdrawals from your investment while steadily reducing the principle amount over time. It calculates the amount you can consistently withdraw from your investment based on your initial deposit, predicted rate of return, withdrawal frequency, and investment term. This enables investors to prepare for retirement income or generate consistent cash flow from investments.

How to Use This SWP Calculator

  1. Enter your total investment amount (₹10,000 to ₹10 crore)
  2. Enter your desired monthly withdrawal amount (₹500 to ₹1 crore)
  3. Input your expected annual return rate (1% to 30%)
  4. Enter your investment time period (1 to 30 years)
  5. View your estimated withdrawals and remaining balance instantly

How does an SWP calculator for monthly withdrawals work?

Our SWP calculator for monthly withdrawals helps you estimate how much you can withdraw each month from your mutual fund investments based on your investment amount, return rate, and time period.

What mathematical approach does this SWP Calculator use?

This SWP calculator uses a monthly compounding formula to calculate the remaining balance after systematic withdrawals. The mathematical formula applied is:

Remaining Balance = Initial Investment × (1 + monthly rate)^n - Withdrawal × [((1 + monthly rate)^n - 1) / monthly rate]

Where:

  • Monthly rate = Annual rate / 12
  • n = Total number of months (years × 12)

The calculation process involves:

  1. Converting the annual return rate to a monthly rate
  2. Calculating the total number of months in the investment period
  3. For each month, applying the monthly growth to the remaining balance
  4. Subtracting the monthly withdrawal amount
  5. The final value after all periods is the remaining balance

Example: For ₹10,00,000 investment, ₹10,000 monthly withdrawal, 12% annual return, 10 years:

  • Monthly rate = 12%/12 = 1% = 0.01
  • Total months = 10 × 12 = 120
  • Using the formula above, the calculator determines the final remaining balance
