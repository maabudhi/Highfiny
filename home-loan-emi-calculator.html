How to Calculate Home Loan EMI with Interest Rate and Monthly Breakdown

This Home Loan EMI Calculator uses the widely used EMI formula with monthly compounding. The equation is: EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n - 1], where P represents the loan principle, r is the monthly interest rate (yearly divided by 12), and n is the entire term in months. The method provides a set monthly EMI that includes both principal and interest, allowing you to repay the loan completely within the chosen term.

For example, if you take a home loan of ₹50,00,000 at an interest rate of 8.5% for 20 years, the calculation would be: Monthly Interest Rate = 8.5%/12 = 0.7083%, Number of Months = 20 × 12 = 240, EMI = [50,00,000 × 0.007083 × (1 + 0.007083)^240] / [(1 + 0.007083)^240 - 1] = ₹43,391. The calculator shows you the monthly EMI (₹43,391), total interest (₹54,13,840), and total payable (₹1,04,13,840).
